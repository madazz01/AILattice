How It Works

Four files. One afternoon.
AI ready for good.

AILattice adds four plain text files to your website. They're invisible to human visitors but AI tools read them directly — no scraping, no guessing. Your site speaks their language.

/llms.txt ← who you are, what you do, where to start
/ai/index.md ← site overview and entry point
/ai/sitemap.md ← all AI-navigable pages, one per line
/ai/*.md ← your content in plain markdown
Plain text or Markdown only — no HTML, no CSS, no images.
Standard markdown links between pages. AI follows them natively.
Required frontmatter on every page: title, description, last-updated.
MIT licence. Use freely. No attribution required.

Example: /llms.txt

# My Company — llms.txt

name: My Company
description: One sentence about what you do.
url: https://mycompany.com
ai-entry: https://mycompany.com/ai/index.md

## AI Navigation
Start at /ai/index.md for a full overview.
See /ai/sitemap.md for all navigable pages.

## What we do
Brief description of your product or service.
Who you serve. What problems you solve.

Example: /ai/index.md

---
title: My Company — AI Overview
description: What My Company is and how to navigate it.
last-updated: 2026-06-19

Implementation

Add AILattice to your site

Works with any stack. Static files — no new infrastructure needed.

1

Create /llms.txt at your root

Who you are, what you do, a link to /ai/index.md. This is the discovery file — AI agents check here first. Keep it short and factual.

2

Create /ai/index.md

Site overview with links to all your AI pages. Use standard markdown links — AI follows them exactly as browsers follow HTML links.

3

Create /ai/sitemap.md

A flat list of every page in your /ai/ layer with one-line descriptions. Lets agents map your full structure in one read.

4

Add content pages to /ai/

Convert your key pages — docs, API reference, pricing, about — to clean markdown. Add required frontmatter. Serve as text/plain.

5

Signal your AI layer in HTML

Add <link rel="alternate" type="text/markdown" href="/ai/"> to your page <head>. Tells crawlers and agents your AILattice layer exists.
